## 3. Purposes and legal bases of processing

We process your personal data for the following purposes, on the following legal bases:

### 3.1. Handling inquiries from the contact form (or e-mail / phone)

- **Purpose**: replying to your inquiry, preparing a quote, discussing terms of a potential transport assignment or vehicle/equipment rental.
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(b) of the GDPR — processing is necessary to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract. Additionally Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR — our legitimate interest in handling business correspondence and maintaining contact with people inquiring about our offer.
- **Scope**: first and last name, e-mail address, phone number, message content, any additional information you choose to provide (planned trip date, pickup and destination, number of passengers, service segment).

### 3.2. Performance of a transport, vehicle rental or event gear rental service

- **Purpose**: conclusion and performance of a passenger transport agreement (Articles 774 et seq. of the Polish Civil Code), vehicle rental agreement (Articles 659 et seq. Civil Code), event gear rental agreement, possible framework agreement with a corporate or institutional client (e.g. care home, foundation, parish).
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(b) of the GDPR — processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which you are a party.
- **Scope**: identification data (name or company name), contact data (phone, e-mail), trip data (date, time, pickup and drop-off, number of passengers, luggage details, any special requirements — e.g. child seat, boarding assistance for a passenger with a disability), data of passengers designated by the client (where the client is not the passenger — e.g. a company booking a transfer for its guest).

### 3.3. Issuing invoices and keeping accounting records

- **Purpose**: documenting the service provided and fulfilling obligations under Polish tax law (Act of 11 March 2004 on Value Added Tax, Act of 29 August 1997 — Tax Ordinance, Act of 29 September 1994 on Accounting).
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(c) of the GDPR — compliance with a legal obligation to which the controller is subject.
- **Scope**: first name, last name, company name, registered or residence address, NIP (Polish tax ID), invoice details, transaction value, subject matter of the service, performance date.

### 3.4. Own marketing (newsletter, special offers) — strictly upon prior consent

- **Purpose**: sending commercial information about our services by electronic means and/or telephone communication for marketing purposes. Own marketing covers only Via Viator services (transport, rental, event gear) — we do not sell or share your data with other entities for marketing purposes.
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(a) of the GDPR — your explicit consent. Additionally Article 10(2) of the Polish Act of 18 July 2002 on the Provision of Electronic Services — consent for sending commercial information by electronic means. Additionally Article 172 of the Polish Telecommunications Act of 16 July 2004 — consent for using telecommunications terminal devices for direct marketing.
- **Scope**: e-mail address, optionally phone number, first name/company name (for personalisation).
- **Withdrawing consent**: at any time, without giving reasons — the "unsubscribe" link in every marketing e-mail or an e-mail to kontakt@viaviator.pl. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before its withdrawal.

### 3.5. Establishing, exercising or defending legal claims

- **Purpose**: establishing, exercising or defending against possible claims arising from concluded agreements or events related to our activity (e.g. damage to luggage, trip delay, complaint, court dispute).
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR — controller's legitimate interest in being able to effectively protect its rights.
- **Scope**: identification and contact data of contract parties, documentation of trip or rental performance (driver's notes, possible correspondence, invoices, vehicle handover protocols).

### 3.6. Analytics and statistics regarding use of the Website

- **Purpose**: understanding how users use our Website, how many people visit, which subpages are read most often — in order to improve service quality and optimise content. We do not build profiles of individual users.
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(a) of the GDPR — your consent expressed in the cookie banner. Analytics is activated solely upon explicit consent. The provider is Plausible Analytics (operator: Plausible Insights OÜ, headquartered in Estonia, infrastructure in Germany) — a privacy-first tool that **does not use cookies, does not collect identifiable IP addresses, does not build user profiles**.
- **Scope**: anonymised website traffic data (country of origin, device type, entry source, time spent on subpage). No personal identifiers.

### 3.7. Website security and abuse detection

- **Purpose**: protecting the Website against attacks (DDoS, brute force, injection attempts), protecting the contact form against spam, identifying and blocking abuse.
- **Legal basis**: Article 6(1)(f) of the GDPR — legitimate interest in ensuring the security of the service and data processed in it.
- **Scope**: technical session data (IP address, user agent, HTTP request timestamps). This data is processed mainly by the infrastructure provider (Vercel) and serves only security purposes.

## 4. Categories of personal data processed

Depending on the relationship with you and the processing purpose, we may process the following categories of personal data:

- **Identification data**: first name, last name, optionally company name.
- **Contact data**: phone number, e-mail address, correspondence address.
- **Invoicing data**: entity name, registered office address, NIP, optionally REGON and KRS for entities registered in the National Court Register.
- **Trip / assignment data**: trip date and time, pickup and drop-off, number of passengers, data of passengers designated by the client, luggage details, special requirements (child seat, boarding assistance, dietary or medical requirements of a passenger — only if the client or passenger volunteers them).
- **Financial data**: settlement amounts, invoice numbers, bank account details (for wire transfers).
- **Technical and session data**: IP address, user agent (browser type, operating system), timestamps, user path data on the site (after analytics consent — in anonymised form).
- **Correspondence content**: e-mail messages, contact form submissions, notes from phone conversations (to the extent necessary for assignment fulfilment).

**We do not process special categories of data** within the meaning of Article 9 of the GDPR (e.g. health data, genetic data, biometric data) — unless you yourself communicate such information to us in connection with an assignment (e.g. information about a passenger's disability for the purpose of arranging an appropriate vehicle or assistance). In that case, the legal basis for processing is Article 9(2)(a) of the GDPR — your explicit consent expressed by knowingly disclosing this information in the assignment context.

**We do not process children's data** in a way that involves knowingly profiling or targeting them with offers. A child's data (name, age, weight — e.g. for child seat selection) is provided by the parent or legal guardian as part of the transport assignment.

## 7. Retention periods (how long we keep data)

We store your data no longer than necessary to achieve the purpose for which it was collected, observing periods required by law.

- **Contact form inquiries and pre-contract correspondence**: up to 12 months from last contact, unless a contract has been concluded — in which case the data becomes part of contract documentation and is retained as per the points below.
- **Data related to contract performance (transport, rental, event gear)**: up to 6 years from the end of the year in which the contract was performed — the limitation period for claims under a contract in business activity according to Article 118 of the Polish Civil Code.
- **Invoices and accounting documents**: 5 years from the end of the tax year in which the tax payment deadline expired — under Articles 86 § 1 and 70 § 1 of the Polish Tax Ordinance and Article 74(2) of the Polish Accounting Act.
- **Data processed for marketing purposes upon consent**: until consent is withdrawn. After withdrawal, data is promptly removed from marketing databases, except for the record of the withdrawal itself, which we keep to demonstrate GDPR compliance.
- **Analytics data (Plausible)**: as per Plausible's policy — aggregated data without person identification, standard retention up to 24 months.
- **Technical logs (Website security)**: up to 12 months, unless a longer period is necessary to investigate a security incident.
- **Data processed for establishing or defending claims**: until the limitation period for the relevant claims expires (usually 6 years), and in the event of proceedings — until their final conclusion.

After these periods, data is deleted or anonymised in a way that prevents linking to a person.

## 8. Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights against Via Viator as the controller of your data:

### 8.1. Right of access (Article 15 GDPR)

You have the right to obtain confirmation from us as to whether we process your data and, if so, access to that data and information about processing purposes, categories of data, recipients, retention periods and rights available to you. On request, we will provide you with a copy of the processed data in a readable form.

### 8.2. Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR)

You have the right to request the rectification of inaccurate data and the completion of incomplete data — to the extent relevant for the processing purposes.

### 8.3. Right to erasure — "right to be forgotten" (Article 17 GDPR)

You have the right to request immediate erasure of your data if one of the grounds listed in Article 17(1) GDPR applies — among others, when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, when you have withdrawn consent and there is no other legal basis, when you have effectively objected, or when the data was processed unlawfully. This right does not apply when processing is necessary for, among other things, compliance with a legal obligation (e.g. invoice retention duty), or for establishing, exercising or defending legal claims.

### 8.4. Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR)

In certain cases you may request restriction of processing — e.g. while we verify the accuracy of data following your rectification request or while we evaluate your objection.

### 8.5. Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR)

With respect to data processed on the basis of consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR) or a contract (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR), processed in an automated way, you have the right to receive it in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format (default: JSON) and to request transmission to another controller, where technically feasible.

### 8.6. Right to object (Article 21 GDPR)

You have the right to object — on grounds relating to your particular situation — to processing of data based on our legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR). In the event of an objection, we will cease processing unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds overriding your interests, rights and freedoms, or grounds for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims.

Regarding processing for direct marketing, you have the right to object at any time, without giving reasons — such objection is absolutely effective.

### 8.7. Right to withdraw consent (Article 7(3) GDPR)

If processing is based on your consent — you can withdraw it at any time, without giving reasons. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before its withdrawal.

### 8.8. Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Article 77 GDPR)

If you believe that the processing of your data by Via Viator infringes the GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority. In Poland this is:

## 9. Profiling and automated decision-making

**Via Viator does not make decisions based solely on automated processing**, including profiling, that produce legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affect you — within the meaning of Article 22 of the GDPR.

In particular:

- **We do not profile** users of our Website to display personalised offers.
- **We do not use creditworthiness scoring algorithms** or similar mechanisms.
- **Decisions on whether to accept an assignment, the price, vehicle and driver availability** are made by a human — usually the operational partner or the designated coordinator.
- **Website analytics** (Plausible) operates exclusively at an aggregate level — it does not build profiles of individual users.

## 10. Voluntariness of data provision and consequences of non-provision

Providing personal data is **voluntary**, but in some situations it is necessary to achieve specific purposes:

- **No contact details** (e-mail, phone) makes it impossible to handle a contact form inquiry, because we have no way to reply.
- **No assignment data** (date, location, number of passengers) makes it impossible to prepare a quote and to perform the transport service or rental.
- **No invoicing data** makes it impossible to issue a VAT invoice — if an invoice is required, its absence hinders settlement.
- **No marketing consent** means we will not send you marketing materials — this does not affect performance of contracts already concluded.

The decision is yours. We do not require more data than necessary for the specific purpose (data minimisation principle, Article 5(1)(c) GDPR).

## 13. Data security

We apply technical and organisational measures ensuring protection of processed data appropriate to the risks and the categories of data covered, in particular:

- **Transmission encryption** — all communication with the Website takes place over HTTPS (TLS 1.3) with HSTS enabled.
- **Strict Content Security Policy** — restriction of script and other resource sources loaded by the Website.
- **Secret hygiene** — access keys to providers (AWS, Vercel, Plausible) are kept exclusively in the infrastructure provider's vault systems, never in source code.
- **Least-privilege access** — access to data is limited to people who need it to perform their duties.
- **Incident response procedures** — in the event of a data breach, we report it to the President of PUODO within 72 hours of discovery, in accordance with Article 33 GDPR. If the breach poses a high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects — we also notify those data subjects (Article 34 GDPR).
